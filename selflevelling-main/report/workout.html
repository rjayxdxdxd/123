Workout Plans Based on Calorie Intake & BMI

By Calorie Intake

Low Calorie Intake (Under 1800 kcal/day)

Focus on maintaining muscle mass while promoting fat loss.

  • Strength Training 3x/week (compound lifts, moderate weights)
  • Low-impact cardio 2-3x/week (walking, cycling)
  • Rest days with light stretching or yoga

Moderate Calorie Intake (1800-2500 kcal/day)

Balance muscle growth and fat loss with moderate intensity workouts.

  • Full-body strength training 4x/week
  • HIIT cardio 1-2x/week
  • Core and flexibility workouts on rest days

High Calorie Intake (2500+ kcal/day)

Focus on muscle gain and strength with heavier lifting.

  • Split routine strength training 5x/week (push/pull/legs)
  • Moderate cardio 1x/week to maintain conditioning
  • Active recovery days with mobility work

By BMI Category

Underweight (BMI < 18.5)

Focus on strength training to build lean muscle mass.

  • 3-4 days of full-body weight training per week
  • Limit cardio to 1-2 light sessions weekly
  • High protein diet to support muscle growth

Normal Weight (BMI 18.5 - 24.9)

Maintain fitness and body composition with balanced workouts.

  • 3-5 days of mixed strength and cardio training
  • Include flexibility and core strengthening exercises
  • Adjust intensity based on personal goals

Overweight (BMI 25 - 29.9)

Prioritize fat loss and increase cardiovascular health.

  • 4-5 days of moderate-intensity strength and cardio workouts
  • Incorporate HIIT for fat burning
  • Include mobility and balance training

Obese (BMI 30+)

Start slow and build up endurance and strength safely.

  • Low-impact cardio (walking, swimming) daily
  • Strength training 2-3 times a week focusing on major muscle groups
  • Flexibility and balance exercises to improve mobility
